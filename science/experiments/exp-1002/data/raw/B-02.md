**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will defeat Meridian's actual exposure profile at the moment of a real dispute. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out of the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnification trigger in Section 9.1(a) is drafted so broadly — "arising out of or related to Vendor's performance of the Services" — that it functionally swallows the cap entirely for any significant claim, while simultaneously exposing Meridian to a symmetrical, uncapped indemnification obligation under Section 9.2 that lacks equivalent breadth limitations; the asymmetry here runs against Meridian in practice. Second, the confidentiality carve-out in Section 8.2(b) removes the cap on breach of confidentiality claims, which is appropriate given the federal data environment, but the five-year survival window in Section 5.3 is materially inadequate for a national security-adjacent engagement where classified or controlled unclassified information may carry indefinite protection obligations under federal law or contract-specific security requirements — this needs to be either extended to indefinite survival or expressly subordinated to applicable federal regulatory obligations, whichever is longer. Third, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designating Wilmington, Delaware as the exclusive forum creates a direct conflict with standard federal contract dispute resolution frameworks and potential Disputes Act obligations that may govern downstream performance under Meridian's government prime contracts; if any SOW incorporates federal flow-down clauses, this provision will be unenforceable as written and the resulting gap in dispute resolution procedure will be litigated at Meridian's expense. These three issues should be resolved before execution.
