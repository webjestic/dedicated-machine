**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will strip Meridian of meaningful protection at the moment of an actual dispute. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out of the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnity itself is triggered only by *third-party* claims — meaning direct losses Meridian suffers from Vendor's breach or negligence without a third-party vector remain subject to the cap, which is pegged to trailing twelve-month fees and will be negligible relative to the scale of a multi-year federal engagement. Second, Section 8.2(b) removes the cap for confidentiality breaches, but Section 5.3 limits confidentiality survival to five years — in a national security data services context, that sunset is structurally inadequate and creates a window where the most sensitive information loses contractual protection entirely while the agreement's other obligations may still be live. Third, the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 mandates Wilmington, Delaware as the exclusive venue with no carve-out for emergency injunctive relief in federal court — in a government contracting environment where data breaches or IP misappropriation may require immediate judicial intervention, this clause as written could be used to delay or defeat emergency relief that Meridian needs before an arbitrator can be seated.
