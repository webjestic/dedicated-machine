**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that create unacceptable exposure for a federal contracting client operating in the national security space. The liability cap tied exclusively to trailing twelve-month fees is structurally inadequate where contract values may be front-loaded, ramp slowly, or where a catastrophic breach occurs early in the engagement — in each scenario, the cap may be nominal relative to actual harm. More critically, the indemnification carve-out in Section 8.2(a) removes the cap on indemnification obligations entirely and without a reciprocal ceiling, which means Client bears unlimited exposure on its own indemnification obligations to Vendor under Section 9.2, including for unauthorized use claims that Vendor could define broadly through SOW scope disputes. Additional deficiencies requiring negotiation before execution include: (1) Section 5.3's five-year confidentiality survival window is insufficient for a national security context — perpetual survival or at minimum a ten-year tail should be required for classified or controlled unclassified information, and the agreement contains no acknowledgment of federal information handling obligations such as CMMC, NIST 800-171, or applicable FAR/DFARS clauses that would govern if federal data is in scope; (2) Section 9.1's vendor indemnification is triggered by "any allegation" of IP infringement but is qualified by the "knowingly infringe" warranty in Section 6.2, creating a gap where Vendor could disclaim a warranty breach while still triggering indemnification exposure on Client's side through a counterclaim posture; (3) Section 10.2's mandatory arbitration clause in Wilmington, Delaware is operationally problematic for a Virginia-based federal contractor and may conflict with dispute resolution mechanisms required under prime contract flow-downs; and (4) the agreement is entirely silent on data security obligations, breach notification, and incident response — an omission that is not merely a gap but an affirmative risk in any engagement touching federal systems or sensitive government information.
