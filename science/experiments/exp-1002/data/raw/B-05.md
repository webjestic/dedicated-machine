**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will strip Meridian of meaningful protection at the moment of a real dispute. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out from the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnity itself is limited to *third-party* claims — meaning direct losses Meridian suffers from Vendor's breach, data failure, or service collapse are capped at twelve months of fees with no consequential damages recovery, which in a multi-year federal engagement will be catastrophically inadequate relative to actual exposure. Second, the Section 8.2(b) carve-out for confidentiality breaches is unlimited in theory, but the confidentiality obligation in Section 5 contains no express carve-out for federal data handling requirements, classified information protocols, or regulatory breach notification obligations — meaning if Vendor mishandles federal data and triggers a compliance event, the confidentiality framework was not drafted to carry that weight and may not be construed to cover it. Third, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designates Wilmington, Delaware as the seat and AAA Commercial Rules as the governing procedure, with no carve-out for injunctive or emergency relief in federal court — in a national security context, Meridian must retain the ability to seek immediate judicial relief without being forced into arbitration procedure first, and this agreement does not preserve that right.
