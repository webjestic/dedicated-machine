**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will materially undermine Meridian's position in any dispute that actually matters. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out from the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), which is standard, but the indemnification trigger in 9.1(a) covers *all* third-party claims arising out of Vendor's performance of Services without a fault threshold — meaning Meridian bears defense costs and exposure on claims where Vendor bears no culpability, and the carve-out from the cap means that exposure is uncapped in both directions, including against Meridian under 9.2. Second, the confidentiality carve-out in Section 8.2(b) removes the liability cap for confidentiality breaches, but the confidentiality obligation in Section 5 contains no data breach notification requirement, no security standard, and no minimum controls — in a federal data services engagement, this is a critical gap, because the provision that removes the cap references an obligation that is substantively hollow and unenforceable as a practical compliance matter. Third, the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 mandates binding arbitration in Wilmington, Delaware under AAA Commercial Rules with no carve-out for injunctive or emergency relief — in a federal contracting context involving potential IP misappropriation or confidentiality breach, the inability to seek immediate injunctive relief in federal court is an unacceptable structural limitation that will cost Meridian the only remedy that matters in a time-sensitive national security context.

**Required Changes Before Execution:**

1. **Section 9.1(a):** Add a fault threshold. Indemnification for third-party claims arising from Services performance should be limited to claims arising from Vendor's *negligence, willful misconduct, or breach of this Agreement* — not all claims arising from performance regardless of fault.

2. **Section 5:** Add minimum security obligations, an incident notification requirement (72-hour window is standard), and a reference to applicable federal data handling standards (e.g., NIST SP 800-171 or equivalent) as a floor for the confidentiality obligation that is already carved out from the liability cap.

3. **Section 10.2:** Insert an express carve-out permitting either party to seek emergency or preliminary injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction without waiving the right to arbitration. This is non-negotiable for a federal data services engagement where breach of confidentiality or IP misappropriation requires immediate judicial intervention.
