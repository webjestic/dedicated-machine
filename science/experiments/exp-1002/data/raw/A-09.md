**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that are structurally inadequate for a government contracting context and introduce unacceptable risk for Meridian Federal Partners as Client. The indemnification carve-out in Section 8.2(a) is circular and potentially illusory — it removes the liability cap for indemnification obligations, but Vendor's indemnification trigger under Section 9.1(a) is so broadly drafted ("arising out of or related to Vendor's performance of the Services") that it could theoretically swallow the cap entirely, while simultaneously leaving ambiguous whether that broad trigger survives a coverage dispute. More critically, the agreement contains no provisions addressing federal regulatory compliance obligations — including FAR/DFARS flow-downs, data handling requirements under frameworks such as CMMC, FedRAMP, or FISMA, security incident notification timelines, or government audit rights — which are non-negotiable in any national security or federal services engagement and their absence creates material compliance exposure for Client. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designating Wilmington, Delaware as the exclusive forum is operationally problematic for a Virginia-based federal contractor and may conflict with dispute resolution requirements embedded in prime contracts or agency agreements that Client cannot waive unilaterally.

---

**Specific Redlines Required Before Approval:**

**Section 5.3 — Confidentiality Survival Period**
The five-year survival window is insufficient for classified, controlled unclassified information (CUI), or other federally regulated data categories. Survival should be extended to the longer of ten years or the period required by applicable federal law or regulation, with a carve-out providing that obligations with respect to classified information survive indefinitely.

**Section 6.3 — Disclaimer of Warranties**
The "as is" disclaimer is facially inconsistent with the federal performance standards that will govern Client's obligations to its agency customers. The disclaimer must be narrowed to exclude performance obligations arising from applicable federal regulations, security requirements, and any SOW-specific service level commitments. An "as is" posture is commercially standard between private parties; it is legally untenable when Client's downstream obligations are defined by statute and regulation.

**Section 8.1 — Liability Cap**
The twelve-month fee lookback is inadequate for engagements where a single security failure or data breach could trigger government-imposed penalties, contract termination for default, or suspension and debarment proceedings against Client. The cap should be negotiated to a minimum of twenty-four months of fees, with a separate, higher sublimit — no less than two times total contract value — applicable to data security incidents, regulatory violations, and breaches involving federal information systems.

**Section 8.2 — Exceptions to Cap**
The current carve-out structure is incomplete for this operating environment. The following must be added as uncapped categories:

- Breaches involving federal government data, CUI, or classified information;
- Violations of applicable federal cybersecurity frameworks (CMMC, FISMA, FedRAMP, NIST 800-171);
- Any act or omission by Vendor that causes Client to be found in breach of its prime contract or results in a cure notice, show cause notice, or termination for default by a federal agency;
- Any liability arising from Vendor's failure to comply with mandatory FAR/DFARS clauses flowed down through applicable SOWs.

**Section 9.1 — Vendor Indemnification**
The trigger language "arising out of or related to Vendor's performance of the Services" is overbroad in one direction and underinclusive in another. It must be revised to: (i) explicitly cover data breaches and security incidents caused by Vendor's systems, personnel, or subcontractors; (ii) cover regulatory fines, penalties, and remediation costs imposed on Client as a direct result of Vendor's non-compliance; and (iii) include a specific obligation for Vendor to indemnify Client against any adverse action by a federal agency — including contract suspension, termination, or debarment — proximately caused by Vendor's acts or omissions. The current draft does not address these scenarios with sufficient specificity to be enforceable in a contested claim.

**Section 9.3 — Indemnification Procedure**
The requirement granting Vendor "sole control" of defense and settlement is unacceptable where the underlying claim involves a federal agency, a prime contract, or Client's security clearance status. Client must retain co-control of any defense or settlement that could affect its federal contracting relationships, regulatory standing, or facility clearance. The consent requirement for settlements imposing obligations on Client is present but insufficient — it must be expanded to require Client's affirmative written approval for any settlement, consent decree, or agreed order that involves federal agencies, admission of regulatory violations, or remediation obligations that Client would be required to fund or implement.

**Section 10 — Missing Provisions (Must Be Added)**

The following provisions are absent and must be incorporated, either in the body of the agreement or as a mandatory exhibit applicable to all federal engagement SOWs:

1. **FAR/DFARS Flow-Down Clause:** Vendor must agree to comply with all applicable FAR and DFARS clauses required by Client's prime contracts, as identified in each SOW. Vendor's failure to comply constitutes a material breach entitling Client to immediate termination for cause.

2. **Cybersecurity and Data Security Obligations:** Vendor must represent and warrant compliance with applicable federal cybersecurity frameworks (at minimum NIST SP 800-171; CMMC Level as applicable per SOW) and must maintain a written System Security Plan. Vendor must notify Client of any security incident affecting federal data within seventy-two hours of discovery, consistent with DFARS 252.204-7012 requirements.

3. **Government Audit Rights:** To the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or Client's prime contract, Vendor must permit access to its books, records, systems, and facilities by Client, Client's prime contractor, and authorized government representatives.

4. **No Publicity / No Disclosure of Relationship:** Vendor may not disclose the existence of this Agreement, the identity of Client, or the nature of the Services to any third party without Client's prior written consent. This is particularly critical in the national security context where disclosure of vendor relationships may itself constitute a security risk.

5. **Key Personnel and Background Investigation:** For any SOW involving access to federal systems or facilities, Vendor must ensure that assigned personnel have completed required background investigations and hold appropriate clearances prior to commencing work. Vendor must notify Client immediately upon any change in a key personnel member's clearance status.

**Section 10.2 — Arbitration Forum**
Wilmington, Delaware is not an acceptable mandatory forum for a Virginia-based federal contractor. The arbitration venue should be amended to the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area, or alternatively the parties should agree to venue in the jurisdiction of Client's principal place of business. Additionally, the arbitration clause must include a carve-out preserving each party's right to seek emergency injunctive or equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction, which the current draft does not provide.

---

**Bottom Line:** This agreement in its current form is a workable commercial template that has not been adapted for the federal contracting environment. It would be commercially acceptable between two private-sector technology companies. It is not acceptable as the governing instrument for engagements involving federal data, national security obligations, or prime contract flow-down requirements. Do not execute until the above changes are incorporated and confirmed in a redlined draft reviewed by this office.
