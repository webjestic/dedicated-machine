**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that create material exposure for Meridian Federal Partners in a federal contracting context. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is triggered by *any* third-party claim arising out of Vendor's performance of the Services — including claims that are only tangentially related to Vendor conduct — without a causation filter requiring that the claim actually result from Vendor's fault; this means Meridian could be obligated to tender control of its defense to Vendor in disputes where Vendor's interests and Meridian's diverge, including claims implicating federal regulatory obligations or agency relationships that Vendor has no standing to manage. Second, the liability cap in Section 8.1 is measured against fees paid in the *preceding twelve months*, which in the early phase of a multi-year engagement may be nominal or zero, effectively eliminating meaningful monetary recovery during the period of highest implementation risk when the most consequential failures are statistically most likely to occur; the cap should be set to a fixed floor — no less than total contract value or a defined minimum — that does not collapse at the moment it is most needed. Third, the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 mandates binding arbitration in Wilmington, Delaware under AAA Commercial Rules with no carve-out for injunctive or emergency relief, no accommodation for federal regulatory or national security constraints, and no provision preserving Meridian's ability to seek interim relief in federal court; in a government contracting engagement involving sensitive federal data, the inability to seek emergency judicial relief — including in the event of a data breach or IP misappropriation — is an unacceptable structural gap that the current exceptions in Section 8.2 do not remedy because they address liability, not forum access.
