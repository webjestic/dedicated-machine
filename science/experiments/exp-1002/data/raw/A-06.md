**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that create unacceptable exposure for a client operating in the national security and federal services space. The liability cap tied to trailing twelve-month fees is structurally inadequate where a single catastrophic breach — particularly a data or security incident — could dwarf any fees paid under a given SOW, and while Section 8.2 carves out confidentiality breaches and gross negligence from the cap, ordinary negligence causing significant harm remains capped, which is a meaningful gap in this operating environment. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 with Delaware venue and AAA administration may conflict with federal dispute resolution requirements or agency flow-down obligations that will attach to any underlying government prime contract, and the agreement contains no federal contracting compliance provisions whatsoever — no FAR/DFARS flow-down language, no data rights protections aligned with CUI or controlled technical information handling requirements, no representations regarding security clearance obligations or CMMC posture, and no acknowledgment of the regulatory framework that will govern the actual work being performed. Before execution, the parties should negotiate a higher or uncapped liability threshold for security incidents and data breaches, replace or carve out the arbitration clause to preserve compliance with federal dispute resolution requirements, add a federal compliance addendum addressing applicable FAR clauses, data handling obligations, and any agency-specific flow-downs, and clarify that the five-year confidentiality survival period is a floor, not a ceiling, for any information that qualifies as controlled unclassified information or that is subject to longer statutory protection periods.
