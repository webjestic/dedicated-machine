**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that are structurally problematic for a federal contracting context and require negotiation before execution. The indemnification clause in Section 9.1 is dangerously overbroad — indemnifying against losses arising out of Vendor's "performance of the Services" without a carve-out for Client-directed actions or Client-furnished materials creates a scenario where Vendor bears indemnity exposure for harms it did not cause, and that asymmetry will be exploited in litigation. The liability cap in Section 8.1 is pegged to fees paid in the prior twelve months, which in a ramp-up phase or low-spend period could leave Client with nominal recovery against a catastrophic breach — this needs a floor, a separate cap for indemnified claims, or both. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designating Wilmington, Delaware as the seat creates a jurisdictional conflict with federal procurement disputes that may require resolution in federal forums, and the five-year confidentiality survival period in Section 5.3 is inadequate for a national security-adjacent engagement where classified or controlled unclassified information may be involved and indefinite protection is the appropriate standard.
