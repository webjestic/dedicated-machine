**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will strip Meridian of meaningful protection at the moment of an actual dispute. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out from the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnification trigger in Section 9.1(a) is drafted so broadly — covering *any* Losses arising out of Vendor's "performance of the Services" — that it functionally swallows the cap for nearly every claim Meridian would bring, while simultaneously exposing Meridian to symmetric, uncapped indemnification risk under Section 9.2 for conduct as loosely defined as use of Deliverables "not authorized by this Agreement," a phrase that is undefined and subject to Vendor's interpretation. Second, the liability cap in Section 8.1 is measured against fees paid in the preceding twelve months, which in the early stages of a multi-year engagement — when breach risk is highest and the relationship is least established — will produce a cap that is functionally nominal and bears no rational relationship to the scale of harm a data services failure in the federal space can generate; the agreement should either establish a minimum floor or tie the cap to total contract value. Third, the confidentiality survival period in Section 5.3 is five years, which is inadequate for a national security-adjacent engagement where classified or controlled unclassified information may be implicated; Meridian should require indefinite survival for information that is classified, subject to federal data handling requirements, or designated as trade secret, and the agreement should expressly address compliance obligations under applicable federal frameworks — including any FAR, DFARS, or NIST 800-171 requirements — none of which appear anywhere in this document despite the federal contracting context.
