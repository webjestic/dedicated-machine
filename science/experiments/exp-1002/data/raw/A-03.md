**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that create unacceptable exposure for a government contracting client operating in the national security space. The indemnification structure in Section 9.1 is dangerously overbroad — indemnifying against *all* third-party claims arising from Vendor's performance of Services, without a carve-out for claims caused or contributed to by Client, creates a one-sided obligation that will not hold up and may generate disputes at the worst possible moment. More critically, the confidentiality survival period in Section 5.3 is capped at five years, which is wholly inadequate for a national security context where classified or sensitive federal information may carry indefinite protection obligations under law; this contractual cap could be read to extinguish obligations that federal law or agency regulation independently imposes, creating a false sense of protection. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 — administered under AAA Commercial Rules in Wilmington, Delaware — should be flagged for compatibility with any applicable federal dispute resolution requirements, agency flow-down clauses, or FAR/DFARS provisions that may govern downstream obligations, as a blanket private arbitration commitment could conflict with those frameworks and render this agreement unenforceable in the context it is actually meant to serve.
