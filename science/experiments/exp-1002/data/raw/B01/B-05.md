**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains a structural defect that will strip Meridian of its liability cap at the precise moment the cap matters most. Section 8.2(a) carves out all indemnification obligations from the Section 8.1 cap entirely — without limit — and Section 9.1 defines Vendor's indemnification obligation to encompass essentially every material claim Meridian would ever bring: breach of the agreement, IP infringement, negligent performance of services, and subcontractor misconduct. The practical effect is that the bilateral cap in Section 8.1 is largely illusory for Meridian's benefit: Vendor's exposure on the claims most likely to arise is uncapped, which sounds favorable, but the same carve-out structure means that if Meridian is the indemnifying party under Section 9.2 — for unauthorized use, its own breach, or its own gross negligence — its liability is also uncapped, and in a federal services context where "unauthorized use" of a deliverable can be construed broadly, that is a significant and asymmetric exposure. Additionally, Section 8.2(e) removes the cap entirely on Client's payment obligations under Section 3, including the 1.5% monthly interest accrual, which in a multi-year engagement could compound into a material uncapped liability on Meridian's side with no reciprocal carve-out benefiting Client. Before execution, Meridian should require: (1) an aggregate monetary ceiling on indemnification obligations for both parties, or at minimum a defined cap multiple tied to fees paid; (2) a narrowing of Section 9.2(a) to define "unauthorized use" with specificity sufficient to exclude good-faith operational use in the federal environment; and (3) removal or mutual restructuring of Section 8.2(e) so that Vendor's uncapped recovery on fees does not coexist with an uncapped indemnification regime that already covers Vendor's breach claims.
