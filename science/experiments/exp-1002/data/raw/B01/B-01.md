**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains a structural defect that will materially harm Meridian in any significant dispute. Section 8.2(a) carves indemnification obligations entirely out of the liability cap — which appears protective — but Vendor's indemnification obligation under Section 9.1 is triggered only by third-party claims, meaning Vendor bears no uncapped direct liability to Meridian for Vendor's own first-party breaches, failures, or negligent performance that cause direct harm without a third-party claim attached. Meridian's direct damages from a Vendor breach remain subject to the twelve-month fee cap under Section 8.1, which in a federal data services engagement of any scale will be wholly inadequate to cover actual exposure from a data incident, service failure, or material non-performance. Additionally, Section 8.2(e) exempts Client's payment obligations from the cap entirely while imposing no reciprocal structural protection for Meridian, and the mandatory arbitration venue in Wilmington, Delaware creates a logistical and jurisdictional friction point that is misaligned with Meridian's federal contracting posture and should be renegotiated to a neutral venue or removed in favor of federal district court.
