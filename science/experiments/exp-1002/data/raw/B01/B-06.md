**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains a structural defect that will strip Meridian of meaningful liability protection in the disputes most likely to arise from a federal data services engagement. The indemnification carve-out in Section 8.2(a) removes the liability cap for indemnification obligations, but Section 9.1 defines Vendor's indemnification duty so broadly — covering *any* third-party claim arising out of Vendor's performance, breach, or subcontractor conduct — that virtually every significant claim against Meridian will be routed through indemnification rather than direct liability, meaning the cap in Section 8.1 effectively applies only to residual, low-value disputes where it was never needed. Critically, this asymmetry runs in both directions: Section 8.2(e) explicitly exempts Client's fee obligations from the cap entirely, while no reciprocal carve-out protects Client's exposure for direct damages from Vendor's service failures, meaning Meridian bears uncapped payment obligations but receives protection only through an indemnification framework it does not control. Before execution, Meridian should require: (1) clarification that the indemnification carve-out in 8.2(a) does not swallow the cap for claims that could be pleaded as either direct or indemnification claims; (2) a hard aggregate ceiling on indemnification exposure for both parties; (3) removal or bilateral restructuring of Section 8.2(e) so that Vendor's direct liability for service failures is not effectively subordinated to Client's uncapped payment obligations; and (4) confirmation that the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 is compatible with any mandatory dispute resolution provisions in the anticipated federal SOWs, which may impose forum or procedural requirements that override this clause.
