**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The liability framework contains a structural defect that will strip Meridian of its cap protection in virtually every dispute that actually matters. Section 8.2 carves out indemnification obligations entirely from the Section 8.1 cap, and Section 9.1's Vendor indemnification obligation is drafted so broadly — covering "any and all" Losses arising from Vendor's performance of Services, breach of the Agreement, or gross negligence — that any significant claim Meridian brings against Vendor will be routed through Section 9 rather than Section 8, rendering the bilateral cap language cosmetic. In practice, this means the cap protects Vendor from direct liability claims while Vendor's indemnification exposure is uncapped, but the inverse is equally true: Meridian's own indemnification obligations under Section 9.2 are also uncapped, meaning a claim that Client breached the Agreement or used Deliverables in an unauthorized manner exposes Meridian to unlimited liability with no ceiling. The agreement presents as symmetrically capped but functions as uncapped in both directions on the claims most likely to arise in a federal data services engagement. Before execution, Meridian should require: (1) a defined aggregate cap applied to indemnification obligations, or alternatively a separate, negotiated indemnification cap expressed as a multiple of fees paid; (2) narrowing of Section 9.1's indemnification trigger so that it does not swallow ordinary breach-of-contract claims that should remain subject to the Section 8.1 cap; and (3) clarification that Section 8.2(e)'s carve-out for fees owed under Section 3 — which is currently unilateral in Vendor's favor — does not operate to create an additional asymmetric exposure for Meridian on payment disputes.
