**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains a structural defect in Section 8.2 that is likely to eliminate the liability cap in every claim that actually matters. By carving out indemnification obligations entirely from the cap — without limiting indemnification to third-party claims or imposing any ceiling on indemnification exposure — Vendor can recharacterize virtually any first-party claim as an indemnifiable event under Section 9.1's broad language ("arising out of or related to Vendor's performance of the Services"), rendering the 12-month fee cap in Section 8.1 functionally illusory for Client. The remaining carve-outs compound this: unlimited liability for IP infringement (Section 8.2(c)) duplicates what is already covered by the indemnification carve-out, creating two independent pathways to uncapped exposure on the same facts, and the gross negligence/willful misconduct carve-out in Section 8.2(d) is unilaterally symmetric in text but asymmetric in practice given the nature of a data services engagement where Vendor controls the systems and Client bears the downstream federal liability. Before execution, Meridian should require: (1) a hard aggregate cap on indemnification obligations, expressed as a multiple of fees paid (typically 2–3×) with a separate, higher sublimit for IP and data breach claims; (2) elimination of the standalone IP carve-out in 8.2(c) as duplicative and structurally dangerous; (3) clarification that Section 9.1's indemnification trigger requires an actual third-party claim and cannot be invoked for first-party losses repackaged as indemnification demands; and (4) consideration of whether the arbitration venue and Delaware governing law create procedural disadvantages in the event of a dispute with federal nexus implications.
