**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that are structurally sound for a commercial context but create unacceptable exposure in a federal contracting environment. The indemnification carve-out in Section 8.2(a) removes the liability cap for all indemnification obligations, which—read alongside the broad Vendor indemnification in Section 9.1(a) covering *any* Losses arising from Vendor's performance of Services—creates effectively uncapped, bilateral exposure with no floor or ceiling defined, a structure that will be exploited in litigation. More critically, the agreement is silent on federal compliance obligations entirely: there is no mention of FAR/DFARS flow-down requirements, data handling obligations under frameworks such as CMMC, FedRAMP, or FISMA, export control compliance (EAR/ITAR), or personnel security clearance requirements—omissions that are not merely gaps but active liabilities in a national security contracting context where regulatory non-compliance can trigger contract termination, debarment, or criminal referral. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 and Delaware governing law in Section 10.1 may conflict with federal dispute resolution requirements or agency-specific contract terms that will govern the underlying prime contract, and the five-year confidentiality survival period in Section 5.3 is almost certainly insufficient for classified or controlled unclassified information that may flow through this engagement.
