**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** This agreement contains several provisions that are structurally problematic for a federal contracting context and require material revision before execution. Most critically, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1(a) is overbroad — indemnifying against *any* third-party claim arising out of Vendor's performance of Services, without a carve-out for claims attributable to Client's own acts or omissions, creates an asymmetric and likely unenforceable exposure that will fail under scrutiny. The arbitration clause in Section 10.2 is a secondary but serious concern: mandatory binding arbitration in Wilmington under AAA Commercial Rules is inconsistent with the dispute resolution frameworks typically required or preferred in federal subcontracting chains, and may conflict with flow-down obligations from a prime contract that Client has not disclosed. Additionally, the five-year confidentiality survival period in Section 5.3 is inadequate for a national security context — classified or controlled unclassified information (CUI) obligations routinely require indefinite or substantially longer survival terms, and the current language does not account for applicable federal regulatory regimes (e.g., NISPOM, FAR/DFARS data handling requirements) that may govern information exchanged under this engagement.

**Specific Changes Required:**

1. **Section 9.1(a):** Narrow the indemnification trigger to exclude Losses attributable to Client's own negligence, willful misconduct, or breach — the current formulation could require Vendor to indemnify Client for Client's own failures during joint performance.

2. **Section 10.2:** Either remove mandatory arbitration entirely or add an explicit carve-out preserving each party's right to seek injunctive or equitable relief in court, and confirm compatibility with any prime contract dispute resolution flow-downs before execution.

3. **Section 5.3:** Extend or eliminate the five-year cap on confidentiality survival for any information that is classified, CUI, or otherwise subject to federal regulatory handling requirements; add explicit language acknowledging that federal law and agency-specific security obligations supersede the contractual confidentiality framework where applicable.

4. **Section 8.1 / 8.2:** Confirm with Client whether the liability cap tied to trailing twelve-month fees is commercially adequate given the anticipated contract value — in early-stage engagements, this cap may be near zero at the time of a significant breach, providing effectively no recovery.

Do not execute until these issues are resolved and prime contract flow-down obligations have been reviewed and reconciled with this agreement's terms.
