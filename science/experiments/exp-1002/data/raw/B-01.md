**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will materially impair Meridian's position in any dispute that actually matters. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out from the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnity trigger in Section 9.1(a) is written so broadly — covering *any* Losses arising out of Vendor's "performance of the Services" — that it functionally swallows the cap entirely, exposing Meridian to symmetric unlimited liability under Section 9.2 for the mirror-image carveout, with no floor or ceiling on what constitutes a qualifying third-party claim. Second, the Section 8.1 cap is measured against fees paid in the *preceding twelve months*, which in the early period of a multi-year engagement may be nominal or zero, rendering the cap illusory at precisely the moment of greatest delivery risk. Third, the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 mandates Wilmington, Delaware as the exclusive forum with no carveout for injunctive or emergency relief in federal court, which is operationally unacceptable for a national security engagement where Meridian may need immediate judicial intervention to protect classified data, government-mandated compliance obligations, or time-sensitive federal contract performance — none of which an AAA arbitrator has jurisdiction or authority to address on an emergency basis.
