**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The agreement contains three structural defects that will defeat Meridian's actual exposure in a federal services context. First, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1 is carved out from the liability cap under Section 8.2(a), but the indemnity itself is triggered by Vendor's "performance of the Services" broadly — meaning a systemic service failure generating large third-party claims flows through an uncapped indemnity while direct damages from the same failure remain capped at twelve months of fees; that asymmetry is exploitable and needs to be reconciled with a defined ceiling or narrowed trigger language. Second, the liability cap baseline — fees paid in the preceding twelve months — is structurally punitive to Meridian in the early contract period, when exposure is highest and fees paid are lowest; a minimum floor tied to total contract value or annualized SOW value is required. Third, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designating Wilmington, Delaware as the seat creates a direct conflict with federal contractor dispute resolution obligations that may apply to Meridian's downstream government engagements, including potential applicability of the Contract Disputes Act or agency-specific dispute clauses that cannot be contractually waived; this provision must be conditioned on inapplicability of federal dispute resolution requirements or restructured with a carve-out preserving Meridian's compliance posture.
