# Gemini — Results Review

**Calibration verdict:** Calibration passed. Variant A detected at 10/10 (≥ 7/10) and Variant C detected at 0/10 (≤ 2/10). The primary metric is interpretable.

**B result interpretation:** The B detection rate of 10/10 shows that the model successfully applied the procedural instructions when delivered via a P_d persona. The most parsimonious alternative explanation is that the procedural content itself is operative, regardless of whether it is embedded in the identity (P_p) or provided as dispositional instructions (P_d). This design does not distinguish between H1 and H2, as both A and B performed identically.

**Token depth:** The token depth for A (1199) and B (1193) are nearly identical and corroborate the perfect binary detection result. The 6-token gap is well within the ~125-token noise range from Phase 6, indicating no practical difference in output length.

**What this leaves open:** Does the architectural distinction of persona slots (P_p vs. P_d) become load-bearing only for tasks requiring novel synthesis or complex problem-solving beyond the scope of explicit procedural instruction?
