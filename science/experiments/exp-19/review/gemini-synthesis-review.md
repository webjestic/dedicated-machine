# Gemini — Synthesis Challenge

**Fatal objection:** The experiment's ceiling effect (A and B both achieving 10/10 success) prevents any observable demonstration of the Persona slot's potential independent architectural lift, thus making the conclusion that it provides no such lift for "identical procedural content" prematurely broad.

**Why it matters:** If the Persona slot offers latent architectural advantages that are only apparent in more challenging, non-ceilinged tasks, then concluding that "content is the operative variable" alone, without acknowledging this limitation, risks misdirecting future research and the formalization of PARC.

**What resolves it:** Replicating the slot-swap experiment with a task specifically designed to be difficult enough that neither A nor B achieves a 10/10 success rate, thereby allowing for the measurement of any differential performance between the Persona and Instruction slots.
