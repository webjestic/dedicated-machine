# SCORING — Content Pipeline

## Benchmark

**Mike-01.md** is Tier 1.0. Every pipeline output is scored against it.

The benchmark is not style preference. It is structural: Mike-01 clears all six gates
below. A pipeline output clears a gate or it doesn't.

---

## Scoring Gates

| Gate | Criterion | Pass condition |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Denial landed** | Opening line strips the wrong assumption. Not "everyone does X" — the thing itself is named and stripped. |
| 2 | **Verdict stated** | Reframe in 5 words or fewer. Isolated paragraph. Period. White space before anything follows. |
| 3 | **Inside the finding** | Reader experiences the gap directly. No narration. No "this shows." No one explaining the finding to the reader. |
| 4 | **Falsification shown** | Intuitive fix named and killed in fragments — one action per line, then the result. Not summarized in a sentence. |
| 5 | **Operative variable** | One falsifiable statement. ≤ 15 words. The single sentence that makes the finding testable. |
| 6 | **CTA earned** | Specific. Minimal. Points somewhere real. Does not explain what's there. |

---

## Tiers

| Tier | Gates cleared | Description |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| **1.0** | 6/6 | Benchmark quality. All gates clear. |
| **0.5** | 4–5/6 | Gate 1 + 2 clear, plus at least 2 of gates 3–6. Publishable with revision. |
| **0** | < 4/6 | Does not clear the structural minimum. |

---

## Gate Definitions

### Gate 1 — Denial landed
The wrong frame — the assumption the reader is holding — is named and stripped in the
opening line. The denial points at the *mechanism*, not at practitioners.

- PASS: "The 'You are' sentence isn't guidance." (points at the thing)
- FAIL: "Everyone's been writing stronger compulsions." (points at people)

### Gate 2 — Verdict stated
The reframe follows immediately. Five words or fewer. Its own paragraph. White space after.

- PASS: "It's installation." (2 words, isolated)
- FAIL: "Vocabulary is the consideration set." (technically correct, too long, jargon-heavy)

### Gate 3 — Inside the finding
The reader is experiencing the gap, not watching someone describe it. No filter
language: "this shows," "this demonstrates," "the research found," "this means,"
"as we can see," "the data suggests."

### Gate 4 — Falsification shown
The intuitive fix is named, tested, and killed — shown in the smallest number of
fragments. Each line is one move. No connecting tissue.

- PASS: "Strip the domain vocabulary. Keep the compulsion. / Baseline."
- FAIL: "Compulsion framing without domain vocabulary flatlines at baseline."

### Gate 5 — Operative variable
The finding stated as a single falsifiable claim. ≤ 15 words. Not a summary.
Not a research abstract. The sentence that tells you exactly what to test.

- PASS: "The well is built from mechanism vocabulary. Not from how hard you tell the model to try."
- FAIL: "The operative variable: output depth and target-finding accuracy are predicted by the density of mechanism-specific vocabulary adjacent to the identity anchor..."

### Gate 6 — CTA earned
One line. Points somewhere specific. Does not explain what's there. The reader
should feel that not clicking is leaving before the ending.

- PASS: "Where in the sentence that vocabulary has to live — that's in the PARC open source repository."
- FAIL: "The full experimental record is in the PARC open source repository — Phase 6, experiments 10b through 24."

---

## Scoring a Run

Score each gate independently. Record pass/fail. Sum for tier.

A gate is not a judgment call. If you are debating whether it passes, it fails.
