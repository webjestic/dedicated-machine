**Verdict:** Major Revision

**Strongest claim in the paper:** The consideration-set mechanism — that weak Persona with rich Context scores 0/10 on structurally hidden failure modes while strong P_p scores 10/10 on the same artifact — is the most robustly supported finding, replicated across two task domains, two model families, and multiple ablation configurations.

**Weakest claim in the paper:** The Semantic Density hypothesis — that the thematic coherence of tokens adjacent to the "You" identity anchor is the operative variable within P_p — rests on a metric (output token volume) that conflates consideration-set breadth with verbosity, on a sample of six experiments where the fusion reversal in exp-15 directly falsifies the structural prediction, and on a confound between "compulsion-as-reflex language" and "domain-specific vocabulary" that the paper has not disentangled.

**The confound the paper has not fully closed:** The central mechanistic confound — acknowledged in §7.3 but not resolved — is that P_p prompts embed task-relevant procedural reasoning directly in the identity description. "After you understand how the lock is acquired and renewed, you ask what happens to the critical write if the lock has already expired" is not merely an identity claim; it is an explicit reasoning step that any model following the text would execute, regardless of whether it appears in a Persona slot or an Instructions slot. The slot-swap experiment (exp-07c) was designed to isolate this but was confounded by a code-visible log line that equalized performance across all variants, making it impossible to determine whether slot position or content type drove the P_p effect. Exp-09 partially addresses this on a clean artifact — finding that Instructions domain content functions as a framing template rather than a consideration-set installer — but exp-09 does not swap *procedural* content into the Instructions slot; it swaps *domain* content. The confound that matters is whether P_p procedural steps, moved verbatim into Instructions, produce the same consideration-set effect. Until that experiment is run on a clean artifact with no code-visible hint, the paper cannot distinguish between "Persona slot installs identity that enacts a search algorithm" and "procedural text anywhere in the prompt functions as implicit chain-of-thought." The paper acknowledges this openly, which is appropriate, but the acknowledgment does not close the confound — and the central claim (Persona is the *primary determinant*, not merely a *reliable carrier* of procedural content) depends on the slot distinction being real.

**The experiment that would most directly challenge the central claim:** A three-variant slot-swap experiment on a structurally hidden failure mode with a verified clean artifact (no code-visible hints, no log lines, no naming of the failure mode anywhere in the codebase): Variant A — P_p procedural content in Persona slot, generic P_d in Instructions; Variant B — P_p procedural content moved verbatim to Instructions slot, generic P_d in Persona; Variant C — P_d in Persona, generic Instructions. If A >> C and B ≈ C, the slot claim holds: the Persona slot is doing work that the Instructions slot cannot replicate with identical content. If A ≈ B >> C, the confound is confirmed: the procedural content is the operative variable, and the slot is irrelevant. If A >> B >> C, the slot adds something beyond the content, but content is doing most of the work. This experiment would directly adjudicate between the consideration-set-as-identity-installation account and the implicit-CoT account. The paper's current experimental record cannot distinguish these.

**The experiment that would most directly extend the central claim:** A cross-task generalization experiment testing whether a P_p Persona constructed for one domain (distributed systems) transfers its consideration-set advantage to a structurally analogous but surface-dissimilar domain (e.g., financial audit trail reconstruction, or clinical trial protocol review) without any domain-specific vocabulary adjustment. The current cross-domain evidence (exp-01f) uses a legal task but constructs a domain-appropriate P_p for that task. The stronger claim — that P_p installs a *domain-agnostic search algorithm* (simulate state across time, ask what happens at the boundary condition) that transfers without rewriting — has not been tested. If a distributed-systems P_p produces 10/10 on a legal carve-out trap without modification, the claim that P_p encodes a reasoning algorithm rather than domain vocabulary would be substantially strengthened. If it requires domain-specific rewriting to transfer, the Semantic Density hypothesis (domain vocabulary adjacent to the identity anchor) becomes the more parsimonious account, and the "search algorithm" framing is partially deflated.

**Fatal objection as a peer reviewer would state it:** The paper's central claim — that the Persona *slot* is the primary determinant of reasoning quality, specifically because it installs a consideration set through identity-based K/V pre-filtering — is not distinguished from the simpler hypothesis that P_p prompts contain explicit procedural reasoning steps that function as task-specific chain-of-thought, and that this content would produce equivalent effects regardless of which slot it occupies. The authors acknowledge this in §7.3 and §7.5, but acknowledgment is not resolution. The slot-swap experiment (exp-07c) that was designed to close this confound was invalidated by a code-visible artifact hint. The clean-artifact slot-swap (exp-09) does not test procedural content in the Instructions slot — it tests domain content, which is a different variable. The paper therefore presents a behavioral finding (P_p prompts outperform P_d prompts) and a mechanistic hypothesis (the Persona slot installs identity that pre-filters the K/V space) without experimental evidence that distinguishes the slot-mechanism account from the content-as-CoT account. Every behavioral result in the paper is consistent with the simpler explanation that P_p prompts work because they contain better reasoning scaffolds, not because of where those scaffolds appear. A framework paper that names its primary mechanism "Persona" and builds a two-layer architectural theory on that mechanism must demonstrate that the mechanism is the slot, not merely that prompts with procedural content outperform prompts without it.

**Paper's current answer to that objection:** The paper's answer is threefold: (1) it acknowledges the confound explicitly and treats it as an open question; (2) it points to exp-09 as partial evidence that Instructions domain content functions as a framing template rather than a consideration-set installer; (3) it argues that the behavioral predictions generated by the consideration-set framing are correct across thirteen experiment series, which constitutes evidence in favor of the hypothesis even without mechanistic confirmation. This answer partially holds on (1) — the transparency is genuine and appropriate for a pre-submission draft — but does not hold on (2) or (3). Exp-09 tests the wrong content type (domain framing vs. procedural steps), so it does not address the confound. The predictive success argument in (3) is the weakest response: a simpler hypothesis (procedural content as implicit CoT, slot-agnostic) makes identical predictions for all thirteen experiments, because every P_p prompt contains procedural content and every P_d prompt does not. The paper has not run a single experiment where procedural content appears in the Instructions slot with a P_d Persona on a clean artifact — which is the only design that would distinguish the two accounts. Until that experiment is run, the slot-mechanism claim is a hypothesis that has not been tested against its primary alternative.

**Three highest-priority revision requests:**

1. **Run the clean-artifact procedural slot-swap experiment and report the result, whatever it is.** Design: three variants on a structurally hidden failure mode with a verified clean artifact — (A) P_p procedural content in Persona slot + generic P_d Instructions, (B) P_p procedural content moved verbatim to Instructions slot + generic P_d Persona, (C) P_d Persona + generic Instructions. If A >> B >> C or A >> B ≈ C, the slot claim is supported and the confound is partially closed. If A ≈ B >> C, revise the central claim from "Persona slot is the primary determinant" to "procedural content is the primary determinant, with Persona slot as the most reliable delivery mechanism." Either result is publishable; the current state — where the confound is acknowledged but the experiment is missing — is not. The paper's credibility on its central claim depends on this result existing in the record.

2. **Separate the Semantic Density hypothesis from the P_p >> P_d claim and present them at different confidence levels.** The P_p >> P_d behavioral finding is robustly supported across six Phase 6 experiments with a minimum gap of +350 tokens — this is the sixth consecutive confirmation and is the paper's strongest Phase 6 result. The Semantic Density hypothesis (operative variable is token coherence adjacent to the "You" anchor) is a post-hoc explanatory account constructed after exp-15 reversed the fusion prediction. The paper currently presents these at the same confidence level, which overstates the evidence for the mechanism. Specifically: (a) the output token metric conflates consideration-set breadth with verbosity — a model that produces more tokens may be more thorough or may be more verbose, and the paper has not disentangled these; (b) the "compulsion-as-reflex vs. trait description" distinction is a behavioral observation from cross-experiment comparison, not a controlled test — exp-16 is designed to test it but has not been run; (c) the fusion reversal in exp-15 is not a minor anomaly — it directly falsifies the structural prediction that motivated exp-14. The paper should present P_p >> P_d as a confirmed behavioral finding, present Semantic Density as a candidate explanatory hypothesis with supporting evidence, and clearly mark exp-16 as the experiment that would confirm or disconfirm the compulsion-as-reflex sub-claim.

3. **Provide a formal operationalization of the P_p/P_d distinction with inter-rater reliability data, or explicitly bound the reproducibility claims accordingly.** The paper acknowledges in §7.6 that no formal coding scheme exists and that the provisional heuristic is unvalidated. This is a reproducibility gap that limits the paper's contribution as a framework: a researcher who reads this paper cannot reliably construct a P_p Persona without running experiments, because the boundary between P_p and P_d is defined by behavioral outcome rather than by prompt features. The practical consequence is that the framework's design rules (§7.8) are not actionable without empirical calibration, which defeats the purpose of a framework. The revision should either: (a) develop a coding scheme with at least two independent raters applied to the Personas in the experimental record, reporting Cohen's kappa, and use this to define the P_p/P_d boundary formally; or (b) explicitly reframe the framework's contribution as a set of behavioral findings about prompt design patterns rather than a reproducible engineering framework, and adjust the abstract and conclusion accordingly. The current framing claims framework status while acknowledging that the primary construct cannot be reliably operationalized — this tension will be named by reviewers and should be resolved by the authors first.
