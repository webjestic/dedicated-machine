**Verdict:** Major Revision

**Strongest claim in the paper:** The behavioral demonstration that weak Persona + rich Context scores 0/10 on structurally hidden failure modes while strong P_p + minimal Context scores 10/10, replicated zero-shot across model families and task domains, establishes that Persona changes the *reachable* reasoning space rather than merely modulating depth within a fixed space.

**Weakest claim in the paper:** The Semantic Density hypothesis — that thematic coherence of tokens adjacent to the "You" identity anchor is the operative variable within P_p — rests on a cross-experiment rank correlation across six experiments with n=10 per variant, where the "sentence quality" judgment used to explain the rank is made post-hoc by the same authors who wrote the sentences, with no independent operationalization and a direct reversal (exp-14 vs. exp-15) that the hypothesis absorbs rather than predicts.

**The confound the paper has not fully closed:** The few-shot / procedural-content confound is acknowledged in §7.3 but not resolved, and the paper's treatment understates how deeply it cuts. The central claim is that the *Persona slot* — specifically, identity framing — installs a consideration set. But every high-performing P_p Persona in the experimental record embeds the target reasoning procedure explicitly in the identity text: "after you understand how the lock is acquired and renewed, you ask what happens to the critical write if the lock has already expired." This is not identity language that happens to produce a search algorithm as a downstream effect; it is the search algorithm written out, wrapped in first-person framing. The confound is therefore not merely that procedural content could be moved to Instructions and produce the same result — it is that P_p as operationalized in this paper *is* implicit chain-of-thought prompting, delivered in the World Layer. Exp-07c was designed to isolate this but was confounded by a code-visible hint, leaving the question open. Until a clean slot-swap experiment on a structurally hidden artifact distinguishes "procedural content in Persona slot" from "procedural content in Instructions slot," the paper cannot claim that the *slot* carries the effect rather than the *content*. The CO-STAR comparison (exp-05) does not close this because CO-STAR's Persona was dispositional by design — it does not control for procedural content in a non-Persona slot.

**The experiment that would most directly challenge the central claim:** A slot-swap experiment on a structurally hidden failure mode with no code-visible hint, using three variants: (A) full P_p with procedural content in Persona slot and generic P_d in Instructions, (B) generic P_d Persona with the identical procedural content moved verbatim to the Instructions slot as a reasoning directive ("When reviewing this code, first understand how the lock is acquired and renewed, then ask what happens to the critical write if the lock has already expired"), and (C) P_d baseline with no procedural content anywhere. If A >> B ≈ C, the slot carries the effect and the consideration-set mechanism is supported. If A ≈ B >> C, the content carries the effect and the paper's central claim reduces to a finding about implicit CoT placement — still useful, but mechanistically different from what is claimed. The paper's current framing predicts A >> B; the confound predicts A ≈ B. This is the experiment that resolves the question the paper acknowledges it cannot currently answer.

**The experiment that would most directly extend the central claim:** A systematic P_p construction experiment varying the *specificity* of the procedural clause while holding domain vocabulary constant — ranging from maximally specific ("after you trace the lock acquisition through the heartbeat renewal cycle, you ask what happens to the pending write if a GC pause freezes the heartbeat thread between renewal attempts") to maximally generic ("after you understand the system's primary operation, you ask what happens if that operation is interrupted at the worst possible moment") — with binary detection scoring on a structurally hidden failure mode. This would establish whether the consideration-set effect is driven by the procedural *structure* (contingent reasoning steps) or by the domain-specific *vocabulary* embedded in those steps, directly testing the Semantic Density hypothesis at the level of individual clause construction and providing the first empirical basis for the P_p operationalization heuristic proposed in §7.6.

**Fatal objection as a peer reviewer would state it:** The paper's central mechanistic claim — that Persona installs a "consideration set" by performing K/V space pre-filtering via procedural identity language — is empirically indistinguishable from the claim that the paper's P_p prompts work because they contain explicit task-relevant reasoning procedures that function as implicit chain-of-thought scaffolds, and that the Persona slot is incidental to this effect. Every P_p Persona in the experimental record contains the target inference written out in first-person form. The one experiment designed to isolate slot from content (exp-07c) was confounded and produced no usable signal. The paper acknowledges this in §7.3 but continues to frame the consideration-set mechanism as a confirmed behavioral finding rather than as one of two competing explanations for the observed P_p advantage. The behavioral observations are real; the attribution to slot-level identity installation rather than content-level CoT is not established. A paper that claims to identify *why* P_p works — and builds a two-layer architectural framework, a unified formula, and a set of mechanistic hypotheses on that claim — must distinguish between these accounts experimentally before submission.

**Paper's current answer to that objection:** The paper's answer in §7.3 and §7.5 is honest but insufficient: it acknowledges the confound, names it as an open question, notes that exp-07c failed to resolve it, and frames the PARC framework as an organizational structure that generated correct predictions rather than a confirmed mechanistic account. This is the right epistemic posture, but it creates a tension the paper does not resolve: the Abstract, Introduction, §3.1.1, §6.1, and §8 all make claims about *why* P_p works (K/V pre-filtering, consideration-set installation, identity-as-search-algorithm) that go beyond what the acknowledged-open confound permits. The paper cannot simultaneously claim in §7.3 that the slot-vs-content question is unresolved and claim in §3.1.1 that "P_p performs K/V space pre-filtering" as an explanation for the behavioral effect. The answer does not hold as currently written — not because the mechanistic hypothesis is wrong, but because the paper presents it as explanatory in the main text and as unresolved in the discussion, and the reader cannot reconcile these two framings without the authors doing that work explicitly.

**Three highest-priority revision requests:**

1. **Resolve the behavioral/mechanistic conflation systematically throughout the main text.** The paper's §6.1 Claim Classification section correctly distinguishes behavioral claims from mechanistic hypotheses, but this discipline does not hold in §3.1.1, §4.2, §4.6, §6.2, and §8, where mechanistic language ("P_p performs K/V space pre-filtering," "installs a search algorithm," "pre-weights the K/V space") is used as explanation rather than hypothesis. Every instance of mechanistic language in the main text must be reframed as a hypothesis that generated correct predictions, not as a confirmed account of what the model is doing. The unified formula in §6.2 should be explicitly labeled a conceptual mapping, not a model of transformer computation — and the formula's use of $P_p^T$ as a literal transpose operation should be either defended as a formal claim or replaced with notation that signals its metaphorical status. This is not a cosmetic change; it is the difference between a paper that accurately represents its evidence and one that will be rejected for overclaiming.

2. **Run the clean slot-swap experiment (exp-07c replication on a hint-free artifact) before submission, or reframe the central claim to what the current evidence supports.** The paper has two options. Option A: run the experiment, report the result, and let it determine whether the claim is "Persona slot installs the consideration set" or "procedural content installs the consideration set regardless of slot." Option B: reframe the central claim as "P_p prompts — operationalized as Persona text containing explicit contingent procedural reasoning steps — outperform P_d prompts on structurally hidden failure modes," which is fully supported by the experimental record, acknowledges the slot-vs-content question as open, and is still a substantial contribution. Option B requires revising the Abstract, §1.1, §3.1.1, §6.2, and §8 to remove slot-primacy language. Option A is preferable but requires one additional experiment series. The paper as currently written takes neither option — it claims slot primacy while acknowledging the confound is unresolved, which is the position a reviewer will reject.

3. **Provide a validated operationalization of the P_p/P_d distinction, or bound the claims that depend on it.** The heuristic in §7.6 ("a Persona is P_p if it contains at least one first-person procedural clause that specifies a reasoning step contingent on a prior state") is unvalidated against the experimental record and has no inter-rater reliability data. This matters for two reasons: (a) reproducibility — a researcher attempting to apply PARC cannot reliably classify a given Persona without running experiments, which makes the framework a black box at the point of use; and (b) the Semantic Density claims in Phase 6 (§4.9, §7.7) depend on ranking Personas by quality using a judgment ("sentence quality," "semantic neighborhood density") that is made post-hoc by the authors with no independent coding. The exp-14/exp-15 reversal — where the same content in fused vs. split form produced opposite orderings across two experiments — is currently explained by appeal to "sentence quality" differences that are not independently measured. Either provide a coding scheme with inter-rater reliability testing applied retrospectively to the experimental Personas, or explicitly bound the Semantic Density claims as preliminary observations requiring validation rather than as a confirmed finding about the operative variable within P_p. The current framing in §4.9 ("The operative variable is semantic neighborhood density") presents a post-hoc explanatory label as a confirmed causal variable, which the exp-14/exp-15 reversal directly undermines.
