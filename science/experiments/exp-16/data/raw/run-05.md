**Verdict:** Major Revision

**Strongest claim in the paper:** The consideration-set mechanism — that Persona type determines *which* failure-mode classes are reachable at all, not merely how deeply the model reasons within a fixed space — is supported by a replicable, cross-domain, cross-model behavioral result (J=0/10 with rich Context across exp-01e and exp-01g; P=1/10 with maximum Stakes in exp-01f) that no alternative explanation in the current draft cleanly accounts for.

**Weakest claim in the paper:** The Semantic Density hypothesis — that the thematic coherence of tokens adjacent to the "You" identity anchor is the operative variable within P_p — rests on a cross-experiment rank ordering of six configurations that reversed direction between exp-14 and exp-15, producing a conclusion ("fusion is not portable; sentence quality is the operative variable") that is itself unfalsifiable without a formal operationalization of "sentence quality" that the paper does not provide.

**The confound the paper has not fully closed:** The few-shot / procedural-content confound (acknowledged in §7.3) is not merely an open question — it is the central confound, and the paper's only direct attempt to close it (exp-07c) was invalidated by a code-visible hint that equalized all variants. The confound is this: every high-performing P_p Persona in the experimental record contains explicit procedural reasoning about the target failure mode embedded in the identity description. "After you understand how the lock is acquired and renewed, you ask what happens to the critical write if the lock has already expired" is, functionally, a one-shot demonstration of the correct reasoning chain, delivered in the first-person identity register rather than the Instructions register. The paper's claim is that the *slot* (World Layer identity) is doing the work; the confound is that the *content* (implicit chain-of-thought) may be doing the work, with the slot being incidental. Exp-09 partially addresses this for the Instructions slot — showing that moving domain content to Instructions produces only a framing template, not a consideration-set installer — but exp-09 does not control for the specific procedural reasoning steps that P_p Personas contain and that Instructions-slot content in the experiment did not contain. The design gap is: no experiment has moved the *procedural reasoning steps* (not just the domain vocabulary) into the Instructions slot on a clean artifact. Until that experiment runs, the slot-hierarchy claim and the consideration-set mechanism claim are both consistent with "implicit CoT in the identity register" as the operative mechanism, which would substantially reframe the paper's contribution.

**The experiment that would most directly challenge the central claim:** A four-variant slot-swap on a clean artifact (no code-visible hints, no log lines, no description-level signals) where: (A) full P_p with procedural steps in the Persona slot; (B) generic P_d Persona with the identical procedural steps moved verbatim into the Instructions slot as a reasoning checklist; (C) generic P_d Persona with the procedural steps moved into the Examples slot as a worked demonstration; (D) P_d baseline with no procedural content anywhere. If B ≈ A >> D, the slot is incidental and the content is doing the work — the paper's central architectural claim collapses into "implicit CoT works, and identity framing is one way to deliver it." If A >> B ≈ D, the slot is load-bearing and the consideration-set mechanism survives. If C >> B ≈ D, the few-shot literature absorbs the finding entirely. The paper cannot be submitted without this experiment, because the current draft acknowledges the confound, names it as the strongest challenge, and then reports that the only attempt to close it was invalidated. A reviewer will not accept "acknowledged open question" as a substitute for the experiment when the experiment is both feasible and directly decisive.

**The experiment that would most directly extend the central claim:** A cross-task-type generalization test holding Persona constant while varying task structure to locate the boundary conditions of the consideration-set effect more precisely. The current evidence establishes the effect on two task types (code review, legal contract review) and one task structure (find the hidden structural failure mode). The extension would test whether P_p's consideration-set advantage persists on tasks where the failure mode is not hidden — where sequential inspection is sufficient — and on tasks where the "failure mode" is not a discrete finding but a distributional property (e.g., identify the systematic bias in a dataset, or the architectural pattern that will cause problems at 10× scale). If P_p's advantage is specific to structurally hidden discrete failure modes (consistent with exp-07c's boundary condition finding), the paper's scope claim should be narrowed accordingly. If P_p's advantage extends to distributional and architectural reasoning tasks, the consideration-set mechanism generalizes more broadly than the current experimental record demonstrates, and the paper's contribution is substantially larger.

**Fatal objection as a peer reviewer would state it:** The paper's central mechanistic claim — that Persona installs a "consideration set" by performing K/V space pre-filtering before the Task Layer is processed — is presented as a hypothesis, but the behavioral evidence the paper offers is equally consistent with a simpler and better-established account: that P_p prompts function as implicit chain-of-thought prompts, and that the identity register is simply an effective delivery mechanism for task-relevant reasoning scaffolds. The paper acknowledges this in §7.3 and §7.5, but the acknowledgment does not resolve the problem — it restates it. Every high-performing P_p Persona in the experimental record contains the target reasoning procedure embedded in the identity description. The slot-swap experiment designed to isolate the slot effect (exp-07c) was invalidated by a confound the authors introduced into the artifact. The paper therefore presents nineteen experiment series in support of a claim that its own meta-reviewer (exp-06) identified as unresolved, and its own experimental record confirms is unresolved, without running the experiment that would resolve it. The behavioral findings are real and interesting. The architectural claim — that the World Layer / Task Layer distinction is the operative variable, rather than the presence or absence of procedural reasoning content anywhere in the prompt — is not supported by the current evidence.

**Paper's current answer to that objection:** The paper's answer is threefold: (1) exp-09 shows that moving domain content to the Instructions slot produces only a framing template, not a consideration-set installer; (2) exp-05 shows that P_p rejects premises that P_d accepts, which is a qualitatively different behavior than CoT depth modulation; (3) the cross-model zero-shot transfer (exp-01g) suggests the effect is encoding a reasoning algorithm, not a Claude-specific activation pattern. This answer does not hold. Exp-09's Instructions-slot content was domain vocabulary, not procedural reasoning steps — the experiment does not test the confound it is cited to address. Exp-05's premise-rejection result is consistent with implicit CoT: a P_p Persona that encodes "I reject the implementation-as-requirement framing on sight" is delivering a one-shot demonstration of premise interrogation in the identity register, and a model following that as a reasoning scaffold would produce premise rejection regardless of whether "identity installation" is a real mechanism. Cross-model transfer is expected under either account — both implicit CoT and consideration-set installation would transfer across transformers. The paper's answer is the best available answer given the current experimental record, which is precisely why the missing slot-swap experiment is fatal: the paper has correctly identified the strongest objection, correctly identified the experiment that would resolve it, reported that the experiment failed due to a confound the authors introduced, and then submitted anyway.

**Three highest-priority revision requests:**

1. **Run the clean slot-swap experiment before submission.** Design a four-variant experiment (P_p full / P_d + procedural steps in Instructions / P_d + procedural steps in Examples / P_d baseline) on an artifact with no code-visible hints, no description-level signals, and no log lines that name or imply the failure mode. Pre-register the artifact and the ground-truth criterion before running. Report the result regardless of direction. If the result closes the confound, the paper's central claim is substantially strengthened. If the result opens it further, the paper's contribution must be reframed as behavioral (P_p prompts outperform P_d prompts) rather than mechanistic (the Persona slot installs a consideration set). Either outcome is publishable; the current state — acknowledged unresolved confound with the resolving experiment not run — is not.

2. **Formally operationalize the P_p/P_d distinction with a validated coding scheme before claiming the hierarchy.** The slot hierarchy claim (Persona > Instructions > procedural AXIOMS > descriptive AXIOMS ≈ Examples) and the Semantic Density hypothesis both depend on the P_p/P_d distinction being reliably applicable by researchers other than the authors. The paper's provisional heuristic (§7.6) is a start, but it has not been validated against the experimental record, has no inter-rater reliability data, and is not sufficient for reproducibility. A minimum viable operationalization: take the Personas from all nineteen experiment series, apply the coding heuristic, compute inter-rater agreement with at least one blind coder, and report the result. If the heuristic cannot reliably classify the Personas that generated the experimental record, the distinction is not yet a scientific construct — it is an authorial intuition that happened to produce correct predictions.

3. **Separate the Semantic Density hypothesis from the confirmed Phase 6 findings, and either run exp-16 before submission or demote the compulsion-as-reflex claim to a design heuristic without mechanistic framing.** The Phase 6 confirmed finding — P_p >> P_d across all six configurations, minimum gap +350 tokens — is robust and should be stated as such. The Semantic Density hypothesis — that semantic neighborhood coherence adjacent to the "You" anchor is the operative variable — is a post-hoc interpretation of a cross-experiment rank ordering that reversed direction between exp-14 and exp-15. The compulsion-as-reflex claim is a further inference from that already-fragile ordering. The paper currently presents all three levels (confirmed finding, post-hoc hypothesis, further inference) in the same register, with the mechanistic framing ("pre-weights the K/V space") applied to the least-confirmed level. Exp-16 (Compulsion Pattern Isolation) is the right experiment; run it, or explicitly label the compulsion-as-reflex claim as a design heuristic pending experimental validation, and remove the mechanistic framing from §3.1.5 and §7.7 until activation-level evidence exists.
