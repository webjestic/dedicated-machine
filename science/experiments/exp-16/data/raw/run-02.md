**Verdict:** Major Revision

**Strongest claim in the paper:** The consideration-set mechanism — that Persona determines which failure-mode classes exist in the model's reachable reasoning space, not merely how deeply it reasons within a fixed space — is supported by a convergent experimental record across two domains, two model families, and multiple ablation designs, with the J=0/10 result (rich Context, weak Persona) as its sharpest empirical anchor.

**Weakest claim in the paper:** The Semantic Density hypothesis — that thematic coherence of tokens adjacent to the "You" identity anchor is the operative variable within P_p — rests on a cross-experiment rank ordering of six configurations where the fusion effect reversed between exp-14 and exp-15, the "compulsion-as-reflex" pattern is a post-hoc observation from uncontrolled wording variation, and the proposed mechanism (K/V pre-weighting by semantic neighborhood) has no activation-level evidence.

**The confound the paper has not fully closed:** The few-shot / procedural-content confound is named in §7.3 and treated as an open question, but the paper's framing understates how severely it threatens the central mechanistic claim. The P_p Personas that drive the 10/10 results contain explicit failure-mode reasoning embedded in the identity description: "after you understand how the lock is acquired and renewed, you ask what happens to the critical write if the lock has already expired." This is functionally a zero-shot chain-of-thought prompt. The consideration-set framing claims that the *slot* matters — that encoding this reasoning as identity rather than instruction produces a qualitatively different effect. But exp-07c, the only experiment designed to test this directly, was confounded by a code-visible log line that equalized all variants and therefore provides no evidence either way. The paper cannot currently distinguish between two hypotheses that make identical behavioral predictions on all completed experiments: (H1) Persona slot installation produces a persistent identity-level prior that pre-filters K/V space before task processing, and (H2) P_p prompts work because they embed task-relevant reasoning steps that any slot would activate if the content were equivalent. Every result in the experimental record is consistent with H2. The paper acknowledges this, but then proceeds to use the mechanistic framing of H1 throughout §3, §4, and §6 — including in the unified formula — without adequately flagging that H1 and H2 are unresolved.

**The experiment that would most directly challenge the central claim:** A clean slot-swap experiment on a structurally hidden failure mode with no code-visible hints, no CI-scope pathway, and no semantic proximity between any Persona well and the artifact's failure type. The design: four variants — (A) P_p procedural content in Persona slot with P_d identity, (B) identical procedural content moved verbatim to Instructions slot with P_d identity, (C) P_p procedural content in Persona slot with P_p identity framing, (D) P_d baseline. If A ≈ C >> D, the slot is inert and the content is doing the work — the consideration-set mechanism is implicit CoT, not identity installation. If C >> A ≈ D, the slot matters and the mechanistic claim survives. The paper's current experimental record cannot answer this question because exp-07c was confounded and no subsequent experiment was designed to replace it. This is the experiment the paper needs before the central mechanistic claim can be stated as anything stronger than a hypothesis.

**The experiment that would most directly extend the central claim:** A prospective calibration study testing whether the P_p/P_d coding heuristic proposed in §7.6 — "at least one first-person procedural clause specifying a reasoning step contingent on a prior state" — predicts consideration-set performance across Personas written by researchers with no knowledge of the framework. The design: recruit N prompt authors, give them a domain and a task, collect their natural Persona constructions, apply the coding heuristic blind, then run each Persona on a structurally hidden failure mode and measure detection rate. If the heuristic predicts performance, the P_p/P_d distinction is operationalized in a way that supports reproducibility claims. If it does not, the distinction remains a post-hoc label applied to prompts that were constructed intuitively and validated behaviorally — which is its current status. This experiment would also generate data on the compulsion-as-reflex hypothesis (§7.7) without requiring a separate exp-16, since natural Persona variation will produce both encoding types.

**Fatal objection as a peer reviewer would state it:** The paper's central claim — that Persona slot identity installation is the primary determinant of reasoning quality — is not distinguishable from the claim that embedding task-relevant procedural reasoning steps anywhere in the prompt is the primary determinant of reasoning quality. The authors acknowledge this confound in §7.3 and §7.5 but do not resolve it, and the paper's experimental design has not produced a single result that discriminates between these two hypotheses. Every experiment in the record is consistent with the interpretation that P_p prompts work because they contain implicit chain-of-thought scaffolding, and that the Persona slot is incidental. The unified formula in §6.2 treats P_p as a mechanistically distinct component that performs K/V space pre-filtering, but this formula is a conceptual mapping with no activation-level evidence, and the behavioral evidence it is grounded in is equally explained by the CoT account. A paper that claims to establish a new architectural principle — World Layer identity installation as categorically distinct from Task Layer specification — must produce at least one experimental result that the alternative account cannot explain. The current record does not contain such a result.

**Paper's current answer to that objection:** The paper's answer is threefold: (1) it explicitly frames mechanistic claims as hypotheses, not confirmed facts, throughout §1.2, §6.1, and §7.5; (2) it argues that the behavioral predictions generated by the consideration-set framing are correct regardless of the underlying mechanism; and (3) it names the few-shot confound as the primary open question and identifies the required experiment. This answer partially holds — the epistemic humility is genuine and the behavioral claims are well-supported — but it does not hold as a defense of the paper's current structure. The problem is not that the mechanistic claims are stated with too much confidence (they are appropriately hedged in the dedicated sections); the problem is that the hedges are localized while the mechanistic framing is pervasive. The unified formula in §6.2, the K/V pre-filtering language throughout §3, the "consideration-set installation" framing in the abstract and conclusion — all of these assert or strongly imply the H1 mechanism in contexts where the hedge has not been repeated. A reader who reads the abstract, introduction, and conclusion without reading §7.3 and §7.5 will come away believing the mechanism is established. That is a structural problem, not a missing citation.

**Three highest-priority revision requests:**

1. **Restructure the mechanistic framing to match the epistemic status of the evidence.** The paper currently hedges mechanistic claims in §1.2, §6.1, and §7.3–7.5, but uses mechanistic language pervasively elsewhere — in the abstract ("installs a consideration set"), the unified formula (P_p as a literal component performing K/V pre-filtering), and the conclusion ("Persona determines the boundaries of the search"). These are not equivalent claims. The revision should either (a) consistently replace mechanistic language with behavioral language throughout, reserving the mechanistic framing for §6 where it is explicitly labeled as hypothesis, or (b) add a standing hedge — e.g., "under the consideration-set hypothesis" — each time the mechanistic framing appears outside §6. The few-shot/CoT confound should be moved from §7.3 (Discussion) to §1.1 (Contributions) or §1.2 (Scope), where it belongs as a primary limitation on the central claim, not a secondary discussion item. The unified formula in §6.2 should be labeled explicitly as a conceptual mapping that assumes H1, with a note that the formula's behavioral predictions hold under H2 as well.

2. **Report exp-07c's confound as a design failure requiring a replacement experiment, not as a completed result with a named limitation.** The current framing — "the slot-swap test was confounded by a code-visible hint" — presents exp-07c as informative about boundary conditions (§7.4) while simultaneously acknowledging it cannot answer the slot-effect question (§7.3). This is inconsistent. The boundary condition finding (P_p advantage is specific to structurally hidden failure modes) is a real result from exp-07c and should be retained. But the paper should be explicit that the slot-swap question — the most important experiment in the record — has not been run on a valid artifact, and that the central mechanistic claim therefore rests on behavioral evidence that does not discriminate between H1 and H2. If the clean slot-swap experiment cannot be completed before submission, the paper should be reframed as establishing the behavioral claims (which are strong) while explicitly deferring the mechanistic claim to future work. Submitting with the current framing invites the fatal objection above from every reviewer who reads §7.3 carefully.

3. **Provide a formal operationalization of the P_p/P_d distinction with inter-rater reliability data, or remove reproducibility claims that depend on it.** The coding heuristic in §7.6 is presented as provisional and unvalidated, which is appropriate — but the paper simultaneously makes strong claims about the P_p/P_d distinction's generalizability ("the procedural specification encodes a reasoning algorithm that any transformer executes," §5) and practical applicability (§7.8 design rules). A researcher attempting to apply Rule 1 ("write the Persona procedurally") cannot determine from the paper whether their Persona qualifies as P_p without running experiments. The Phase 6 results make this more acute: exp-14 and exp-15 show that the same content in fused vs. split form produces different results in different directions depending on wording, which means even experienced practitioners cannot predict P_p strength from inspection. The revision should either (a) provide the inter-rater reliability data needed to validate the coding heuristic, (b) replace the design rules with empirical calibration guidance ("run your Persona on a known-difficulty artifact before deploying it"), or (c) explicitly scope the design rules as heuristics for practitioners who will validate empirically, not as specifications that can be applied by inspection. The self-prediction gap finding (§7.2) directly contradicts the implied usability of the design rules as written — this tension should be resolved, not left for the reader to notice.
