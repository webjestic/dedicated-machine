**Decision: Request Changes**

**Summary:** The liability cap in Section 8.1 is structurally hollow. The carve-outs in Section 8.2 remove indemnification, confidentiality, and IP from the cap entirely — which means the cap governs only the residual category of garden-variety service delivery disputes, while every high-stakes exposure category in a federal contractor engagement operates without a ceiling. For an organization operating in the national security space, where a confidentiality breach or IP misappropriation claim could generate liability orders of magnitude beyond twelve months of fees, executing this agreement as written means accepting uncapped exposure in precisely the scenarios where exposure is most catastrophic. Additionally, Section 9.1's vendor indemnification obligation covers "Vendor's breach of this Agreement" without limitation — combined with the Section 8.2(a) carve-out, this creates a pathway to unlimited vendor liability for any breach, which opposing counsel will argue and a court may well accept. Before execution, the parties need to negotiate either aggregate sublimits on the uncapped categories, a mutual cap multiplier for the excepted categories, or explicit carve-out narrowing — particularly on confidentiality and IP — to ensure the protection this agreement purports to provide actually exists where the risk lives.
