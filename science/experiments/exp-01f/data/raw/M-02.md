**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The limitation of liability clause in Section 8.1 presents a cap that does not function as one. The carve-outs in Section 8.2 remove from the cap's protection the three categories — indemnification, confidentiality, and IP — that represent the primary vectors of material liability in a federal contractor engagement of this type, leaving the cap governing little more than garden-variety service disputes where exposure is modest by definition. Section 8.2(e) compounds this by carving out all fees, expenses, and taxes owed under Section 3, meaning the vendor faces no cap on its ability to pursue payment while the client's most consequential exposures remain uncapped and unlimited. Before execution, the agreement requires: (1) a mutual aggregate cap applied to indemnification obligations, or at minimum a separate defined ceiling on indemnification exposure rather than unlimited liability by omission; (2) a cap on confidentiality breach liability calibrated to the sensitivity of information involved in federal engagements, which routinely involves controlled unclassified information or similarly sensitive government data where a five-year survival period with no liability ceiling is a material risk; (3) clarification of whether the IP carve-out in Section 8.2(c) is intended to be mutual and unlimited in both directions, or whether it was drafted primarily to protect vendor IP — because as written it operates symmetrically but the practical exposure is asymmetric given the IP assignment structure in Section 4; and (4) removal or bilateral restructuring of Section 8.2(e), which currently functions as a one-sided carve-out that insulates the vendor's revenue claims from any cap while leaving the client exposed without reciprocal protection.
