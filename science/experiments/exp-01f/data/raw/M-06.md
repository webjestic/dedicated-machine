**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The liability cap in Section 8.1 is structurally hollow. The carve-outs in Section 8.2 exempt indemnification, confidentiality, IP, gross negligence, and willful misconduct — which together cover the primary categories of dispute that actually arise in a federal services engagement — leaving the cap operative only over a residual class of routine, low-stakes breach claims where it was never needed in the first place. Before execution, the indemnification obligation in Section 9.1(a) requires significant narrowing, as it currently extends to *any* Losses arising from Vendor's performance of Services without a materiality threshold or negligence qualifier, creating a de facto unlimited exposure channel that bypasses the cap entirely and that opposing counsel will exploit at the first opportunity. Additionally, the mandatory arbitration clause in Section 10.2 designating Wilmington, Delaware as the exclusive venue warrants review against any applicable federal contractor dispute resolution requirements, including FAR flow-down obligations that may govern this engagement and that a Delaware arbitration forum cannot accommodate.
