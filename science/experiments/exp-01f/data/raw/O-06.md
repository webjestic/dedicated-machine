**Decision: Request Changes**

**Summary:** The limitation of liability clause in Section 8.1 presents a 12-month fee cap as meaningful bilateral protection, but Section 8.2 carves out indemnification, confidentiality, IP, gross negligence/willful misconduct, and all unpaid fees — which is to say, it carves out every category of dispute that generates material exposure in a software professional services engagement. What remains inside the cap is a narrow residual of ordinary contract performance claims where damages are unlikely to be significant in the first place. For your client, the practical effect is asymmetric: Vendor's indemnification obligations under 9.1 are unlimited and uncapped, which sounds favorable, but Client's own exposure under 9.2 — for unauthorized use of Deliverables or breach — is equally unlimited, and in a federal contractor context where data handling, IP chain of title, and compliance obligations are live issues, that is not a theoretical risk. Before execution, I would require: (1) a defined aggregate ceiling on indemnification obligations for both parties, or at minimum a negotiated cap multiple (e.g., 3× trailing fees) applied to indemnification claims, to prevent the indemnification carve-out from functioning as a de facto unlimited liability exposure for Client; (2) clarification that Section 8.2(c) on IP is limited to third-party infringement claims and does not silently expand to cover first-party IP disputes between the contracting parties, which the current language does not restrict; and (3) review of whether the "as incorporated" license in Section 4.3 for Vendor Pre-Existing IP is sufficient for the federal contractor use case, given that government flow-down requirements may demand broader rights than a non-exclusive internal use license provides.
