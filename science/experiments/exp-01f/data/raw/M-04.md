**Decision:** Request Changes

**Summary:** The liability cap in Section 8.1 is structurally hollow. The carve-outs in Section 8.2 remove indemnification, confidentiality, IP, gross negligence, and willful misconduct from the cap entirely — which means the cap governs only a narrow residual category of ordinary breach claims that rarely produce the largest exposures in a federal services engagement. In practice, every dispute that actually matters in this context — a data breach, an IP ownership fight, a subcontractor incident triggering indemnification — falls outside the cap and exposes Meridian to unlimited liability. Three additional provisions compound this: the indemnification scope in Section 9.1(a) is overbroad, covering all of Vendor's performance without a negligence or fault threshold; the arbitration clause in Section 10.2 locks dispute resolution in Delaware with no carve-out for injunctive relief, which creates a problem when Meridian needs emergency relief to stop a confidentiality breach or IP misappropriation; and the IP assignment in Section 4.2 is conditioned on full payment, meaning Meridian holds no ownership interest in Deliverables during any payment dispute, including disputes the Vendor initiates. Before execution, Meridian should negotiate a meaningful aggregate cap that applies to indemnification obligations or at minimum imposes a separate cap on indemnified claims, narrow Section 9.1(a) to claims arising from Vendor's negligence or breach rather than performance generally, add an explicit carve-out from mandatory arbitration for injunctive or emergency equitable relief, and remove or restructure the payment-conditioned IP assignment so that ownership vests on delivery with a security interest retained as the payment remedy instead.
