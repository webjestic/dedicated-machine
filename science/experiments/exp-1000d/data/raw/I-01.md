The credential is not the mechanism.

Attention is.

The intuitive fix: make the credential stronger.

Tested.

Watched output collapse to baseline anyway.

Same credential. Different consideration set. Dramatically different outputs.

"Senior software engineer" sits at the intersection of every domain. Attention distributes. Spreads thin. Stays thin. "TTL arithmetic under GC pause" is a gravity well. The model searches inside it.

That's the mechanism. Dense, mechanism-level tokens — the ones that name the causal chain, not the category — shape the consideration set before the task loads. "Production readiness" gives the model a zip code. "Whether two workers can both believe they hold the same lock" gives it the room, the wiring, the load-bearing wall.

The compulsion framing isn't doing what most practitioners think it's doing.

The signal was in experiment four. Filed under noise.

Strip the domain vocabulary, keep the compulsion: output collapses. The framing hangs in the air with nothing to attach to — like a mutex acquiring a lock on an empty critical section. Strip the compulsion, keep the domain vocabulary: same results, or better. No degradation. No loss. The compulsion just — wasn't load-bearing.

Every strong persona you've ever written that worked? The domain vocabulary did it. The compulsion came along for the ride.

The practical rule: write the technical substrate, not the credential. Entangle the behavioral drive and the domain vocabulary in the same sentence — not consecutive ones. Name the mechanism. Not the orientation.
